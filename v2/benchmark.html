Benchmark

Per-run, per-subscore results for the graded reconstruction runs on T20S. The scoring scheme behind these numbers — every subscore and exactly how it is computed — is on the Grader page; the runs themselves, with their prompts, job graphs, maps, and full reports, are on the Tasks page.

T20S MVP · public data 4 graded runs + degraded controls weights provisional · calibration pending snapshot 2026-06-25

Four end-to-end runs are graded, spanning the two action surfaces — the agent calls constrained pipeline jobs, or writes pipeline code directly — and two guidance levels (fully scaffolded, or tutorial-/brief-only). A random-decisions floor and a set of deliberately degraded maps are included as anchors and controls. Every subscore is a value in [0, 1], higher = better; a cell shown n/a returned no data and dropped out of its block — its siblings absorb the weight — rather than scoring zero.

Each outcome block is reported twice: measured — the raw quantity the subscore reads (Å, correlation, area, ratio) — and normalized — that quantity mapped to [0, 1] and combined by weight into the block.

Resolution is each run's own loose-mask gold-standard FSC at the 0.143 threshold, recomputed under one fixed mask for every map.

Three same-subset reference points anchor the outcome scores. Ceiling — an expert's by-hand reconstruction of the same 20 movies (≈3.15 Å); defines map quality 1.0. Floor — a minimal pipeline with random interactive decisions on the same movies (≈4.35 Å); defines map quality 0.0. Deposit — the published full-data reconstruction (EMD-6287, 2.8 Å) and atomic model (PDB 6BDF); a held-out reference above the subset ceiling, never the normalizer.

Procedure

Weight 0.50. Decision quality from the run's job graph and interactive choices, independent of final resolution. Two groups, weighted equally as on the Grader page: trajectory (0.50) and interactive jobs (0.50). Trajectory splits into tree completeness (0.70) and tree similarity (0.30); interactive jobs splits into select-2D (0.50 — a template pass and a refine pass, 0.50 each) and particle picks (0.50). Each interactive-jobs leaf is therefore 0.25 of procedure.
